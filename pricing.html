Buyer Costs & Fees Guide

Clear, upfront pricing for buyers participating in auctions on NovaBid.

Purpose

This guide explains the types of costs and fees buyers may encounter when using NovaBid. Our goal is full transparency: all applicable costs are disclosed before you bid or commit to a purchase.

How Buyer Pricing Works

NovaBid is designed to minimize friction for buyers while maintaining marketplace trust and security. Depending on the auction and vendor requirements, your total cost may include more than just the winning bid.

You will always see a complete cost breakdown before you place a bid or submit payment.

What Buyers May Pay

1. Winning Bid Amount

The winning bid is the primary cost of any purchase. If you win an auction, this amount becomes the base price paid to the vendor.

2. NovaBid Platform Fee (If Applicable)

In some cases, NovaBid may apply a buyer platform fee to support marketplace operations and security. This fee, if applicable, is clearly displayed before checkout.

Some vendors choose to absorb this fee, resulting in no platform fee for buyers.

3. Vendor-Defined Costs or Premiums

Vendors may apply additional, clearly disclosed costs such as:

  • Buyer’s premiums
  • Shipping or delivery fees
  • Handling or administrative charges
  • Taxes or regulatory fees

These costs are set by the vendor and are always disclosed before bidding begins.

Payment Methods & Processing Fees

Payment methods vary by vendor and auction configuration. Supported options may include:

  • Bank transfer or ACH
  • Credit or debit cards
  • Cryptocurrency (if supported by the vendor)
  • Escrow services for select transactions

Any processing fees charged by payment providers or card networks are passed through transparently and are not marked up by NovaBid.

Verification Requirements & Fees

To protect buyers and vendors and reduce fraud, NovaBid supports identity and payment verification.

Payment Method Verification

Verifying a payment method results in a $1 charge. This charge is used solely to confirm the validity of the payment method and is not a purchase. In most cases, the charge is reversed or credited by the payment provider.

Required Verification

The majority of vendors on NovaBid require some level of buyer verification in order to participate in their auctions. Required verification may vary by auction and may include identity or payment checks.

Any verification requirements are clearly disclosed before you are allowed to bid.

Buyer Protections

NovaBid emphasizes transparency and accountability:

  • All fees and costs disclosed upfront
  • Immutable bid and event records for auditability
  • Clear dispute resolution based on verified auction data
  • Optional escrow for higher-value transactions

Why This Matters

Many auction platforms introduce hidden premiums or post-auction fees. NovaBid takes a different approach: transparency first.

Buyers always know exactly what they are paying before they commit.

  3. Review auction details and fee disclosures
  4. Bid with full cost visibility
